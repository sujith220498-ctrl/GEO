# WorkOnGrid — Search & AI Visibility Assessment
April 2026

**Tools used:** DataForSEO API, Perplexity AI (live prompts), Google Search
**Note:** Google Analytics and Google Search Console for workongrid.com were not accessed in this assessment. Traffic figures are DataForSEO estimates based on keyword rankings and standard click-through rate models. Actual traffic may differ.

---

## What This Covers

Where workongrid.com stands today in organic search and AI-generated recommendations — the two channels where B2B buyers increasingly form their first impression before any human contact.

---

## 1. Organic Search: Current State

The site generates approximately 2,660 estimated organic visits per month across India and the US.

**India (75% of traffic):** Most India traffic is branded — "on grid", "ongrid login", "work on grid". These are existing users, not new buyers discovering the product.

**US (21% of traffic):** Comes almost entirely from blog content. The homepage and product pages contribute less than 10% of organic traffic.

**The three use case pages** — Transformer Health Monitoring, OCR Meter Reading, AI-Powered Revenue Assurance — have zero keyword rankings. They are live but invisible in search.

## 3. AI Visibility: Current State

Five prompts that WorkOnGrid's buyers are likely using in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini were tested live. WorkOnGrid appeared in **0 of 5 responses**.

| Prompt | Who was recommended |
|---|---|
| Best AI platform for DISCOMs in India | Bidgely (first recommendation) |
| Best software to reduce AT&C losses | No specific vendor named |
| Best transformer health monitoring software | IBM Maximo, Schneider Electric, GE Predix |
| Alternatives to Fluentgrid MDMS | Oracle, BCITS, Trilliant |
| Best smart meter analytics platform | Itron, Grid4C, Kamstrup |

When a DISCOM CTO consults an AI assistant before shortlisting vendors, WorkOnGrid is not in the conversation. A preference is already partially formed by the time they contact anyone — and it is not WorkOnGrid.

---

## 4. Why the Gap Exists

LLMs do not primarily cite company websites. Over 97% of AI citations come from third-party sources — review platforms, industry publications, comparison roundups.

**WorkOnGrid is listed on Capterra.** It is not listed on:
- Gartner Peer Insights — where "alternatives to Fluentgrid" queries pull from
- aimultiple.com — cited for "best smart meter solutions" queries
- startus-insights.com — cited for emerging utility tech queries
- tndindia.com, mercomindia.com, electricalindia.in — the India-specific publications Perplexity cited

Bidgely earned the #1 DISCOM India recommendation because a single article on tndindia.com covered their product launch. That one third-party page was enough.

---

## 5. What Needs to Happen

**Immediate — no content creation required:**
- Submit to Gartner Peer Insights (free listing, 2–3 weeks to go live)
- Get listed on aimultiple.com and startus-insights.com smart meter / utility tech roundups
- Pitch a product coverage article to tndindia.com, mercomindia.com, electricalindia.in — the exact sources Perplexity is already citing

**Short term — content creation required:**
- Build "WorkOnGrid vs Fluentgrid" comparison page — directly captures the highest-intent competitive query
- Consolidate blog content around each use case into one authoritative guide per use case — structured so LLMs can confidently cite it
- Update the energy data management blog post — 60,500 monthly searches, rank 79. One optimisation pass on this page has significant traffic upside

**Ongoing:**
- Run a 10-prompt LLM audit monthly across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini
- Refresh blog content every 4–5 months — content older than this decays in both Google rankings and LLM citations
- Track which third-party pages competitors appear in and ensure WorkOnGrid is on those same pages
