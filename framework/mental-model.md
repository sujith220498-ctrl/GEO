# Mental Model: Evidence Architecture

## The Core Reframe

Old model: You build trust directly with buyers. Your content, your deck, your sales team — you control the narrative because buyers come to you.

New model: An intermediary (the LLM) forms a verdict about you before the buyer ever reaches you. That verdict is assembled from evidence scattered across the web — mostly from sources you don't own. By the time the buyer arrives, the belief is already partially formed.

**You are no longer marketing to buyers. You are marketing to the systems that shape buyer belief before buyers know they have a belief.**

---

## What This Changes

Old frame: publish content → rank → get traffic → convert.

New frame: build a body of evidence across owned and third-party sources → give the model enough material to assemble a confident recommendation → trust arrives before the sales interaction.

---

## The Three-Part Structure

**1. AI is a synthesis engine, not a search engine.**
It doesn't return results. It assembles an answer from what it trusts. The question isn't "how do I rank in ChatGPT?" It's "what evidence environment am I building for the model to draw from?"

**2. Third-party credibility outweighs self-reported credibility.**
97.8% of citations come from domains you don't own. This mirrors how humans form trust — what others say about you carries more weight than what you say about yourself. Your own site is a starting point, not the foundation.

**3. Neglect is a signal.**
Stale content doesn't just decay in rankings. It signals to the model that you don't care about accuracy — which is precisely what the model is trying to surface. Freshness is a proxy for trustworthiness.

---

## The Closest Analogy

This is analyst relations, not content marketing.

In analyst relations, you don't control the Gartner report. But you brief analysts, get cited in the right quadrants, and shape what the industry's trusted intermediaries say about you. That third-party endorsement is what moves enterprise deals.

LLMs are now playing that role at scale, for every buyer, at every stage. The brands that treat GEO as content marketing will optimise the wrong thing. The brands that treat it as analyst relations — building relationships with the sources the model trusts — will build the evidence environment that produces recommendations.

---

## Citation Supply Theory

A brand with 15 citable pages gives the model 15 reasons to include them.
A brand with 4 gives it 4.

More source material → higher model confidence → more recommendations → trust arrives before the sales call → shorter deal cycles.

---

## Two Levers

**Own-site:**
Content that ranks on Google for bottom-funnel, product-intent keywords gets cited by LLMs. Structure matters — schema, author pages, FAQs, comparison pages, use cases by role and industry. Content older than 5–6 months starts to decay.

**Third-party:**
Identify which external pages LLMs are already pulling from in your category — review sites, niche roundups, comparison articles. Get your brand placed on those exact pages. Not for backlinks. For brand mentions on the URLs LLMs already trust.
